What are HABs?

SonicSeaSentinel is pioneering eco-friendly technology to combat the adverse effects of harmful algal blooms (HABs). Our innovative approach combines machine learning and ultrasonic technology to create scalable, non-invasive solutions for HAB detection and mitigation.

Our team is dedicated to protecting aquatic ecosystems by eliminating HABs that threaten marine biodiversity, water quality, and public health. Our comprehensive approach is designed to identify HABs in large-scale water bodies, making our solution a practical alternative to traditional, chemical-based methods that often harm other marine life.

Our Mission

Our mission is to offer an effective, environmentally safe approach to controlling harmful algal blooms. By integrating drone-based detection and targeted ultrasonic emissions, we strive to preserve marine life and promote healthier waterways.

We are committed to sustainability and innovation. Our solutions are designed not only to combat current HABs but also to contribute to long-term environmental stewardship, reducing the need for hazardous chemicals and minimizing ecological disturbances.

Mapping & Monitoring

SonicSeaSentinel utilizes drone technology and satellite imagery to monitor harmful algal blooms in real-time. By capturing high-resolution images and environmental data, our drones help us track bloom growth and map affected areas with precision.

Through a combination of machine learning and satellite data from programs like Sentinel-2, we ensure comprehensive coverage. This approach allows us to quickly identify and respond to bloom activity, ensuring timely mitigation while keeping a watchful eye on ecosystem health.

Our Technology

Our two-step process leverages drones for precise detection and ultrasonic transducers for effective mitigation of harmful algal blooms.

Our aerial drone component is equipped with high-resolution sensors to capture detailed environmental images and data. These drones can autonomously scan vast areas of water, identifying HAB hotspots and marking their locations for treatment.

The ultrasonic transducer module, designed for underwater deployment, emits sound waves at precise frequencies. These frequencies are tuned to effectively target and disrupt the cellular structure of specific algal species, leaving the surrounding ecosystem unharmed.

Our Research

Harmful algal blooms (HABs) pose significant threats to aquatic ecosystems, public health, and local economies by degrading water quality, causing fish kills, and producing harmful toxins. Traditional methods of detecting and mitigating HABs are often labor-intensive and expensive, targeting only smaller water bodies. Additionally, many detection systems currently work on a microbial scale or focus only on detection rather than species classification.

Existing research indicates that ultrasonic waves at specific frequencies can effectively destroy algal cells, but current devices are limited to utilizing a fixed ultrasonic frequency. This project leverages machine learning to locate and classify multiple types of oceanic HABs, utilizing ultrasonic emissions at varied frequencies to eliminate each targeted HAB.

The system analyzes satellite images with convolutional neural networks to identify and categorize different types of harmful algae, allowing for rapid, large-scale monitoring of water bodies and providing timely data for environmental management. After the visual processing, we identify the optimal ultrasonic wave frequency for each identified HAB and emit it through a transducer. Our technique ensures that other marine life is not affected by accounting for the hearing frequency ranges of various ocean-dwelling organisms.

We develop a prototype consisting of two parts: a drone for autonomous scanning across the ocean, and a flotation device for treatment. Our prototype successfully combines satellite-based detection with ultrasonic mitigation, demonstrating the practical application of this dual approach. Preliminary field tests indicate the prototype's effectiveness in identifying and reducing oceanic HABs. This scalable and eco-friendly solution improves HAB management by supporting multiple species and applying ultrasonic mitigation towards the ocean, contributing to the protection of aquatic environments and public health.
